In javascript, when we want to encode, we may use encodeURI and encodeURIComponent. What is the difference between them?

characters they do not encode

encodeURI will not encode the following 82 characters: !, #, $, &, ', (, ), *, +, ,, -, ., /, :, ;, =, ?, @, _, ~, 0-9, a-z, A-Z

encodeURIComponent will not encode the following 71 characters: !, ', (, ), *, -, ., _, ~, 0-9, a-z, A-Z

It is obvious that encodeURIComponent will encode more characters than encodeURI including : and /. So encodeURIComponent will encode http:// to http%3A%2F%2F, while encodeURI will not.

situations where they can be used

When you encode the whole URL, choose encodeURI:

encodeURI("http://www.cnblogs.com/season-huang/some other thing");
//"http://www.cnblogs.com/season-huang/some%20other%20thing"

When you encode parameters of URL, choose encodeURIComponent, especially when the parameter contains /:

var param = "http://www.cnblogs.com/season-huang/";
param = encodeURIComponent(param);
var url = "http://www.cnblogs.com?next=" + param;
console.log(url);
//"http://www.cnblogs.com?next=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.cnblogs.com%2Fseason-huang%2F"
